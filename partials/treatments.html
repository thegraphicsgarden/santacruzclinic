Cupping

Cupping is a healing modality that uses small glass cups as suctions devices that glide across the skin or stay stationary. The result is the equivalence of an inverted massage. Instead of applying pressure to a specific area cupping gently lifts muscles upward. The negative pressure created by the cups helps draw blood and energy to the target area, which helps loosen muscles and sedate the nervous system. This gently and relaxing treatment is beneficial for releasing toxins, common cold, asthma, stiff muscles, migraines, fatigue, cellulite, and much more!

Moxa

Moxibustion is a Chinese medicine therapy that uses the burning of mugwort to facilitate the healing process. There are different methods of burning mugwort, directly on the skin with salt, garlic, or ginger or indirectly manually or on a need. The smell is similar to the burning of an incense and the sensation is similar to a warm hug. Studies show that moxa over specific areas can help active immune functions and increase red and white blood cell count, enhance lymphocyte and other natural killer cell activity and Lower blood pressure. Moxa also helps autoimmune disorders, chronic pain, digestive issues, fertility and gynecological issues, injuries, arthritis, carpal tunnel, nerve inflammation, KD and GB stones, and more!

Acupuncture

Acupuncture originated in China around 2000-60000 BC. Some believe it was first developed during the Stone Age in china. The essence of acupuncture is the belief a type of life force, energy, or “breath” called QI that flows throughout the body in energy pathways called meridians. Each meridian corresponds to a specific organ, or a group of organs, that govern certain bodily functions. Qi disharmonies cause disease and illness. Achieving harmonious flow of qi is thought to help create and maintain health and wellness. The insertion of very thin needles in specific acupuncture points restores the balance of qi and facilitates well being.
